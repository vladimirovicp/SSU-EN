    Saratov Region (Russian: Сара́товская о́бласть, Saratovskaya oblast) is located in the southeast of European Russia, in the northern part of the Lower Volga region, 858 km southeast of Moscow.

Preparatory department for foreign citizens

We accept applicants all year round
  • Pre-university training program (the Russian Language + other subjects)

    This program is targeted at foreign citizens who do not speak Russian and wish to study undergraduate (Bachelor) and graduate (Master) programs at SSU or other universities.

    This program includes the study of the Russian Language (starter/beginner level) and 3 general educational subjects, depending on/according to the direction of the future specialty, i.e. where students plan to apply to in the future.

    The list of majors (educational program specialization) and the list of general educational subjects depending on the profile of the future specialty:

    • Humanitarian/Humanities (History, Social Studies, Literature)
    • Economic (History, Social Studies, Mathematics)
    • Medical and Biological (Biology, Chemistry, Physics)
    • Natural Science (Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry)
    • Engineering, Technical and Technological (Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science)
    • Total hours: 1012 contact hours
    • Mode of study: full-time / via use of exclusively distance learning technologies (online mode)
    • Fee: 112,332 rubles / 101,200 rubles
    • Start: upon arrival from September 1 to June 30, from November 1 to August 31
    • Deadline for the program: December 5

Admission

Traditionally, Saratov State University enrollment campaign begins on June, 20th and ends at the end of August.

If you are a foreign citizen, a person without citizenship, or a compatriot living abroad, provide the following documents when applying:

  • А copy of the applicant identity document, or an identity document of a foreign citizen in the Russian Federation

  • Russian translation of the applicant identity document, or an identity document of a foreign citizen in the Russian Federation certified as required by RF Law

  • Оriginal foreign educational certificates and (or) academic certificates and qualifications

  • Russian translation of original foreign educational certificates and academic record transcript certified as required by RF Law

  • Сopies of documents or other evidence supporting affiliation of a compatriot living abroad to the groups provided by the Article 17 of the Federal Law dated May 24, 1999 No. 99-FL “On the National Policy of the Russian Federation towards Compatriots Living Abroad”

  • 6 photos

The applicant’s surname, name and patronymic (if any), indicated in the translations of the submitted documents, shall comply with the surname, name and patronymic indicated in the identity document of a foreign citizen in the Russian Federation.

If you have any further questions concerning procedures associated the enrolment campaign at Saratov State University, feel free to contact for further detailed information.:

Residence

  • Invitation letter/ Pre-arrival information for representatives from visa countries

    Processing of an invitation letter for entry into the Russian Federation for foreign citizens

    General information:

    • For obtaining a study visa, the foreign citizen’s passport expiration date should not be less than 18 months from the date of intended/planed entry.
    • Scanned copy of passport should be clear and easy-to-read.
    • When a foreign citizen is going to apply for a Russian visa in a country differing from the country of citizenship, a copy of the document confirming the right of the foreign citizen for permanent stay in a third country and a notarized translation of this document should be attached to the documents submitted for processing an invitation letter. The document is meant to be valid for 6 months after the future Russian visa expiration.
    • An invitation for entry into the Russian Federation for foreign citizens is issued in electronic form and sent to the e-mail put on the application form when submitting documents.
    For submission of documents for invitation processing, you need:
    1. Send an original passport scan (the first page with a photo) to balaeva.mal.sal@gmail.com;
    2. Send a scan of the latest Russian visa (if the foreign citizen previously visited Russian Federation);
    3. Fill in the application form at https://forms.gle/GErH2Gw63RNo5osd6;
    4. For invitations on humanitarian visas (scientific and technical cooperation), as well as for foreign citizens arriving to study at the SSU Preparatory Department, a state fee of 800 rubles is paid.The state fee is paid to the banking details.
  • Registration upon arrival (necessary for all categories of foreign citizens)
